# Route: the prime and squarefree-semiprime model

Select a set \(S\) of prime vertices and a graph \(E\) whose edge \(pq\)
means that the squarefree semiprime \(pq\) is selected.  In this restricted
universe, admissibility is exactly:

1. \(E\) is \(K_4\)-free; and
2. \(E[S]\) is triangle-free.

Three semiprime--semiprime representations of a squarefree four-prime
product are its three perfect matchings and require all six edges of a
\(K_4\).  Three prime--semiprime representations on three primes require
the triangle and all three selected vertices.  Adding a dummy vertex joined
to \(S\) combines the two conditions into \(K_4\)-freeness.

Taking all primes and all semiprimes crossing the split at \(\sqrt n\)
is admissible and has size
\[
\frac n{\log n}(\log\log n+1+B_1+o(1)).
\]
This correctly explains the leading term and the baseline secondary
coefficient.  It is not extremal: higher-\(\Omega\) coefficient profiles
and, more strongly, variable compatible fibers improve the \(n/\log n\)
term.  Thus the graph route survives as a normalized restricted model, not
as the final construction.
