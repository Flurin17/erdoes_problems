# Route: multiplicative energy

For \(R=\binom{|A|}{2}\), let \(d\) be the number of products with exactly
two represented pairs.  Under admissibility,
\[
|P^\wedge(A)|=R-d,\qquad
\sum_m r_A(m)^2=R+2d\leq2R.
\]
The exact obstruction is instead
\[
\sum_m\binom{r_A(m)}3=0.
\]

Ordinary second energy cannot determine the secondary constant.  Complete
bipartite prime-product families are admissible and asymptotically saturate
the energy/support inequalities, leaving no fixed gap to exploit.  Local
ratio-section identities remain useful for deriving fiber constraints, but
the standalone global-energy route was demoted.

First failure point: the inference that admissibility should force
\(|P^\wedge(A)|\geq(1/2+\varepsilon)R\) for a fixed
\(\varepsilon>0\) is false.  Bipartite constructions have
\(|P^\wedge(A)|/R\to1/2\).
