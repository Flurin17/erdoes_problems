# Primitive long circuits for the internally distinct reading

Let (p_0,\ldots,p_s) be distinct primes and (Q=\prod_{j=0}^sp_j). Set
\[
A_i=Q/(p_{i-1}p_i)\quad(1\le i\le s),\qquad
B_j=Q/p_j\quad(1\le j<s).
\]
Every endpoint prime occurs (s-1) times on either side and every internal
prime occurs (s-2) times, so
\[
\prod_{i=1}^sA_i=\prod_{j=1}^{s-1}B_j.
\]
All (2s-1) factors are globally distinct and squarefree. A subidentity
using (a) long-side and (b) short-side factors would satisfy
(a(s-1)=bs); coprimality of (s-1,s) makes the circuit primitive.

If (p_j\in(P,2P)), (N=\lfloor Q/P\rfloor), and
(s\asymp P/\log P), then every factor lies in ((N/(4P),N]), while
(P\asymp\log N). Hence the circuit lies in
((N/(C\log N),N]) and has support
(\asymp\log N/\log\log N\). It obstructs naive top-slab constructions but
does not itself force a positive-density transversal.
