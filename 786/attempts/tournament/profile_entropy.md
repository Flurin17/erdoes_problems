# Exact-budget entropy and martingale bounds for prime-band colors

## Result

Let (f) be any completely additive function on the integers up to (N),
let (c\in\mathbb Q), and put

\[
 A_c=\{n\leq N:f(n)=c\},\qquad
 \alpha=|A_c|/N,
 \qquad \delta=1-\alpha.
\]

The lemmas below give an exact information budget for any partition of the
primes into size bands.  They do not replace the uniform measure on
([1,N]) by a product measure: all completion counts retain the remaining
multiplicative size budget.

The main quantitative consequence is the following.  At a prime-band node,
suppose two sets of possible band factors each have exact conditional mass at
least (\beta), and the two sets lead to eventual level-(c) probabilities
whose means differ by at least (\gamma).  If (W_j) is the uniform mass of
nodes with this property at band (j), then

\[
 \boxed{\quad
 \delta(1-\delta)\ \geq\
 \frac{\beta\gamma^2}{2}\sum_j W_j .
 \quad} \tag{1}
\]

Thus a total positive mass of genuinely separated color branches over the
bands gives a positive-density complement.  Formula (1) is an unconditional
finite lemma; the remaining number-theoretic bottleneck is to force such
branches for every nonzero additive grading.

## Exact band fibers

Partition the primes at most (N) into disjoint sets
(P_1,\ldots,P_J).  (They may, in particular, be consecutive size bands.)
Let (\mathcal S_j) be the multiplicative monoid generated by (P_j),
including (1).  Every (n\leq N) has a unique representation

\[
 n=b_1\cdots b_J,\qquad b_j\in\mathcal S_j.
\]

Consequently uniform (n\in[1,N]) is exactly uniform on the down-set

\[
 \Omega_N=\left\{(b_1,\ldots,b_J):b_j\in\mathcal S_j,
             \ \prod_{j=1}^Jb_j\leq N\right\}. \tag{2}
\]

For (T\geq1), (t\in\mathbb Q), and (1\leq j\leq J+1), define

\[
 \begin{aligned}
 Z_j(T)&=\#\left\{(b_j,\ldots,b_J):
                \prod_{i=j}^Jb_i\leq T\right\},\\
 Z_j(T,t)&=\#\left\{(b_j,\ldots,b_J):
                \prod_{i=j}^Jb_i\leq T,
                \ \sum_{i=j}^J f(b_i)=t\right\}.
 \end{aligned} \tag{3}
\]

For the empty suffix, set (Z_{J+1}(T)=1) and
(Z_{J+1}(T,t)=\mathbf1_{t=0}).  These are finite, literal counting
functions, not Euler-product approximations.

Let (B_j=b_j(n)), let
(\mathcal F_j=\sigma(B_1,\ldots,B_j)), and set

\[
 H_j=\Pr(n\in A_c\mid\mathcal F_j).
\]

If a prefix has product (d=b_1\cdots b_{j-1}), color
(s=\sum_{i<j}f(b_i)), and remaining budget
(T=\lfloor N/d\rfloor), then exactly

\[
 H_{j-1}=\frac{Z_j(T,c-s)}{Z_j(T)}. \tag{4}
\]

Conditional on this prefix, the next band factor (b\in\mathcal S_j),
(b\leq T), has probability

\[
 \pi_{j,d}(b)
 =\frac{Z_{j+1}(\lfloor T/b\rfloor)}{Z_j(T)}, \tag{5}
\]

and, after that choice, its exact continuation success probability is

\[
 h_{j,d}(b)
 =\frac{Z_{j+1}(\lfloor T/b\rfloor,c-s-f(b))}
        {Z_{j+1}(\lfloor T/b\rfloor)}. \tag{6}
\]

In particular, (6) keeps both pieces that are lost in a naive independent
band model: the shifted additive target (c-s-f(b)) and the new size budget
(\lfloor T/b\rfloor).

## Lemma 1: exact relative-entropy budget

Let (\mathbb P) be uniform measure on ([1,N]) and let
(\mathbb Q=\mathbb P(\,\cdot\mid A_c)), assuming (0<\alpha<1).  For every
ordering of the prime bands,

\[
 \log\frac1\alpha
 =\sum_{j=1}^J
   \mathbb E_{\mathbb Q}
   D\!\left(
      \mathcal L_{\mathbb Q}(B_j\mid\mathcal F_{j-1})
      \,\middle\|\,
      \mathcal L_{\mathbb P}(B_j\mid\mathcal F_{j-1})
   \right). \tag{7}
\]

Equivalently, at every prefix having (H_{j-1}>0),

\[
 \mathbb Q(B_j=b\mid\mathcal F_{j-1})
 =\pi_{j,d}(b)\frac{h_{j,d}(b)}{H_{j-1}}, \tag{8}
\]

and the (j)-th summand in (7) is

\[
 \mathbb E_{\mathbb Q}\log\frac{H_j}{H_{j-1}}. \tag{9}
\]

In particular, Pinsker's inequality gives the quantitative estimate

\[
 \sum_{j=1}^J\mathbb E_{\mathbb Q}
 \left\|
      \mathcal L_{\mathbb Q}(B_j\mid\mathcal F_{j-1})
      -\mathcal L_{\mathbb P}(B_j\mid\mathcal F_{j-1})
 \right\|_{\rm TV}^{,2}
 \leq\frac12\log\frac1\alpha. \tag{10}
\]

### Proof

Bayes' formula gives (8), since (H_j=h_{j,d}(B_j)) and
(H_{j-1}=\mathbb E_{\mathbb P}(H_j\mid\mathcal F_{j-1})).  Therefore the
conditional log likelihood ratio is
(\log(H_j/H_{j-1})), proving (9).  On (A_c), (H_J=1), while
(H_0=\alpha).  Hence the log likelihood ratios telescope pointwise there:

\[
 \sum_{j=1}^J\log\frac{H_j}{H_{j-1}}
 =\log\frac1\alpha.
\]

Taking (\mathbb Q)-expectations proves (7).  Each conditional expectation
in (9) is a relative entropy and hence nonnegative.  Applying
(D(\mu\|\nu)\geq2\|\mu-\nu\|_{\rm TV}^2) in (7) proves (10). \(\square\)

For a putative density-(1-o(1)) level, the right side of (10) is (o(1)),
not merely (o(J)).  Thus exact-level conditioning cannot substantially
bias the next-band law at a positive total mass of scales.

## Lemma 2: exact leave-one-band concentration

Fix any one prime band (P\), and put all primes outside (P) first and
(P) last.  Let (\mathcal U) be the monoid generated by the outside
primes.  For (u\in\mathcal U), (u\leq N), set

\[
 \begin{aligned}
 \mathcal V_u&=\{v\leq N/u:\text{ every prime factor of }v\text{ lies in }P\},\\
 M_u&=|\mathcal V_u|,\\
 r_u&=|\{v\in\mathcal V_u:f(v)=c-f(u)\}|,\\
 \theta_u&=r_u/M_u.
 \end{aligned} \tag{11}
\]

Thus (u\mathcal V_u) is the exact fiber allowed by (uv\leq N), and
(r_u) is its level-(c) part.  Under the distribution of (u) induced by
a uniform element of (A_c), namely

\[
 \mathbb Q_U(u)=\frac{r_u}{\alpha N}, \tag{12}
\]

one has

\[
 \mathbb E_{\mathbb Q_U}\log\frac1{\theta_U}
 \leq\log\frac1\alpha. \tag{13}
\]

More precisely, there is the exact decomposition

\[
 \log\frac1\alpha
 =D(\mathbb Q_U\|\mathbb P_U)
  +\mathbb E_{\mathbb Q_U}\log\frac1{\theta_U},
 \qquad
 \mathbb P_U(u)=\frac{M_u}{N}. \tag{14}
\]

Consequently, for every (0<\eta<1),

\[
 \mathbb Q_U(\theta_U\leq1-\eta)
 \leq
 \frac{\log(1/\alpha)}{-\log(1-\eta)}. \tag{15}
\]

There is also the purely counting bound

\[
 \mathbb Q_U(\theta_U\leq1-\eta)
 \leq\frac{\delta(1-\eta)}{\alpha\eta}. \tag{16}
\]

### Proof

The fibers (u\mathcal V_u) partition ([1,N]).  Conditional on (u),
(\mathbb P) is uniform on all (M_u) choices of (v), whereas
(\mathbb Q) is uniform on the (r_u) choices having the one required band
color (c-f(u)).  The conditional relative entropy is therefore
(\log(M_u/r_u)=-\log\theta_u).  The chain rule for relative entropy and
(D(\mathbb Q\|\mathbb P)=\log(1/\alpha)) give (14), hence (13) and (15).

For (16), let (M_0=\sum_{r_u=0}M_u).  Direct counting gives the exact
identity

\[
 \delta N
 =M_0+\sum_{r_u>0}r_u\left(\frac1{\theta_u}-1\right). \tag{17}
\]

On (\theta_u\leq1-\eta), the factor in parentheses is at least
(\eta/(1-\eta)).  Divide the resulting inequality by
(\alpha N) to obtain (16). \(\square\)

Thus, if (\delta=o(1)), then for every chosen band and for almost every
level element (in the exact (A_c)-weighted sense), fixing all outside prime
exponents leaves a band fiber whose required additive color occupies
(1-o(1)) of all configurations allowed by the actual residual size budget.
This is substantially stronger than a statement about an unconditioned
Euler product.

## Lemma 3: multiscale separated-branch penalty

At a prefix node (x=(b_1,\ldots,b_{j-1})), regard (5) as a probability
law (\pi_x) on the possible next band factors and (6) as a function
(h_x:\mathcal S_j\to[0,1]).  Suppose there are disjoint sets
(U_x,V_x\subset\mathcal S_j) such that

\[
 a_x:=\pi_x(U_x)\geq\beta,
 \qquad b_x:=\pi_x(V_x)\geq\beta, \tag{18}
\]

and

\[
 \left|
 \mathbb E_{\pi_x}(h_x\mid U_x)
 -\mathbb E_{\pi_x}(h_x\mid V_x)
 \right|\geq\gamma. \tag{19}
\]

Call such a prefix ((\beta,\gamma))-separated, and let

\[
 W_j=\mathbb P(\text{the prefix before band }j
                   \text{ is }(\beta,\gamma)\text{-separated}). \tag{20}
\]

Then (1) holds:

\[
 \alpha(1-\alpha)
 \geq\frac{\beta\gamma^2}{2}\sum_{j=1}^J W_j. \tag{21}
\]

In particular, since (\alpha(1-\alpha)\leq\delta),

\[
 N-|A_c|\geq
 \frac{\beta\gamma^2}{2}
 \left(\sum_jW_j\right)N. \tag{22}
\]

### Proof

The process ((H_j)_{j=0}^J) is the Doob martingale of
(\mathbf1_{A_c}).  Orthogonality of martingale differences gives

\[
 \sum_{j=1}^J
 \mathbb E_{\mathbb P}
 \operatorname{Var}_{\mathbb P}(H_j\mid\mathcal F_{j-1})
 =\operatorname{Var}_{\mathbb P}(\mathbf1_{A_c})
 =\alpha(1-\alpha). \tag{23}
\]

At a fixed prefix, (H_j=h_x(B_j)) and (B_j\) has law (\pi_x).  For any
random variable (Y), two disjoint events of probabilities (a,b) and
conditional means (\mu_U,\mu_V) satisfy

\[
 \operatorname{Var}(Y)
 \geq\frac{ab}{a+b}(\mu_U-\mu_V)^2. \tag{24}
\]

Indeed, condition on the three-part partition (U,V,(U\cup V)^c), use
the law of total variance, and minimize the variance of the three
conditional means over the third mean.  The minimum is the right side of
(24).  From (a,b\geq\beta),

\[
 \frac{ab}{a+b}=\frac1{a^{-1}+b^{-1}}\geq\frac\beta2.
\]

Hence every separated prefix contributes at least
(\beta\gamma^2/2) to its conditional variance.  Average over prefixes,
sum over (j), and use (23) to obtain (21).  Since
(1-\alpha=\delta) and (\alpha\leq1), (22) follows. \(\square\)

## What the lemma reduces the open step to

Equations (18)--(19) are entirely finite and use the exact counts (3).
They can be tested without pretending that prime bands are independent.
For instance, one may take (U_x,V_x) to be two substantial classes of
band factors with different additive colors.  Their continuation means are
then averages of the two *different exact atoms*

\[
 \frac{Z_{j+1}(\lfloor T/b\rfloor,c-s-f(b))}
      {Z_{j+1}(\lfloor T/b\rfloor)}.
\]

The proved conclusion is that a density-(1-o(1)) level can exist only if,
over every prime-band filtration, almost all such substantial color
alternatives are hidden by near-equality of these exact continuation atoms;
quantitatively, their total separated-node mass is (o(1)).  Lemma 2 says
simultaneously that the last-band atom is (1-o(1)) on almost every
level-weighted exact fiber.

The weakest unsupported step is now precise: prove that for every nonzero
prime-weight profile producing the level (c\ne0), some ordering into size
bands forces

\[
 \sum_jW_j\geq w_0,
 \qquad \beta\geq\beta_0,
 \qquad \gamma\geq\gamma_0
\]

for absolute positive constants, or derive the same conclusion after
grouping several adjacent bands.  By (22), that would give the desired
absolute density gap.  A direct falsification test for any proposed version
of this forcing statement is the threshold grading
(f(n)=\sum_{p>N^{1/u}}v_p(n)): all (Z_j(T,t)) can be enumerated exactly,
and any claimed (\beta_0,\gamma_0,w_0) must remain valid for that profile,
whose level density is already known to be about (0.828499) at its optimum.
