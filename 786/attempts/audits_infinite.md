# Audits of the infinite obstruction

## Grading and quantifiers

Certified. The length map factors through valuation vectors, including
one-sided relations because \(1\notin A\). Extension to the algebraic direct
sum is valid, infinite support is harmless, and no global denominator is
needed. The conclusion bounds lower or existing natural density, not upper
natural density alone.

## Compound-Poisson and winding

Certified. For \(s>1\),
\[
 \sum_{p,k\geq1}\frac{p^{-ks}}k=\log\zeta(s)<\infty.
\]
Finite Lévy truncations converge in total variation and each has its own
common denominator. Their characteristic functions have winding zero; an
atom above \(1/2\) at a nonzero target forces nonzero winding. Zero targets
and deterministic drift are correctly excluded.

## Density and finite exponent

Certified. Partial summation has the correct direction for lower density.
The finite extension and rearrangement inequality are valid. Optimizing
\(e^{-\beta c}-e^{-c}\) gives
\((1-\beta)\beta^{\beta/(1-\beta)}\) and the unique stated root. The
conclusion is \(m+1\geq N^{\beta_*-\varepsilon}\) eventually for every
\(\varepsilon>0\), not a sharp exact exponent.

## Independent route

Three additional audits certified a finite-prime harmonic factorization and
geometric fair-bit/Sperner argument giving a weaker universal density gap.
They checked zero-atom coverage, finite-prime normalization, order of limits,
and the finite-support sieve branch.
