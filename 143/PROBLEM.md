# Erdős Problem #143

Source: <https://www.erdosproblems.com/143>  
Fetched from the site's LaTeX-source view on 2026-07-16.

## Verbatim statement

Let $A\subset(1,\infty)$ be a countably infinite set such that for all $x\neq y\in A$ and integers $k\geq1$ we have
\[
|kx-y|\geq1.
\]
Does this imply that $A$ is sparse? In particular, does this imply that
\[
\sum_{x\in A}\frac1{x\log x}<\infty
\]
or
\[
\sum_{\substack{x<n\\x\in A}}\frac1x=o(\log n)?
\]

## Initial goal

Convert multiplicative separation into quantitative occupancy bounds on dyadic or logarithmic intervals strong enough for one or both sparsity conclusions.
