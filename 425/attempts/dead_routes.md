# Precisely eliminated shortcuts

1. **Higher-\(\Omega\) elements are negligible.** False.  A fixed smooth
   kernel times a \(C_4\)-free prime graph gives target-order many elements
   \(2pq\), all with \(\Omega=3\).

2. **Ordinary multiplicative energy characterizes the problem.** False.
   Equations \(ab=c^2\) are allowed because diagonal pairs are excluded.

3. **A raw divisor graph must be \(C_4\)-free.** False.  Only \(C_4\)'s with
   four distinct edge labels correspond to forbidden collisions.

4. **Independent smooth-kernel bounds can be summed.** False as a method.
   Their formal weights diverge; layers must be coupled before applying
   \(C_4\) estimates.

5. **Arbitrary cofactor fibers have a prime-sized quotient alphabet.** False.
   Coprime doubletons already give \(\Theta(z^2)\) disjoint quotient spectra.

6. **All leading semiprime mass lies near \(\sqrt n\).** False.  Reciprocal
   annuli at many scales add their leading coefficients.

7. **Aggregate two-path inequalities certify the Bellman constant.** False as
   a proof method.  A two-level fractional profile beats it while satisfying
   all such inequalities; realizability is a higher-order design question.

8. **Largest-prime incidence girth suffices for fixed \(r\).** False.
   Smooth families such as \(uv^2\) have forest incidence graphs but retain
   multiplicative rectangles invisible to that graph.

9. **One-at-a-time prime exchange tensors.** False.  The sets
   \(\{2p,2q\}\) and \(\{1,pq\}\) exhibit the two additional simultaneous
   blocker types.
